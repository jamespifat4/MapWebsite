Definition of a mesh block: A mesh block showcase the pieces that make up the smallest geographical areas. They in turn, generate the steps to make greater regions of Australia. Mesh blocks are demarcated by the Australian Bureau of Statistics, whilst the larger regions statistics are formulated by the Australian Statistical Geography Standard. The primary role of mesh blocks is to locate the boundaries of practicality within urban or rural areas. Meaning to display how high human activity levels are among many factors like use of roads, how buildings accessed along them, where residents are and arent, industrial/ primary production area locations, park areas, major facilities like hospitals and transport routes. Moreover, the underlying aspects that reflect Australias socio-economics can be seen through where high-density housing is, locations of higher and lower income etc, whilst also exhibiting gender and age variations among the factors that make mesh blocks. Such as, the gender ratio working in the CBD.

Census: A census is an official survey ordered by the government of any country, to discover the details of a nations population and each individuals demographics. The Australian census began nationally in 1911. It occurs every five years, examining data such as gender, age, occupations, income, dwelling types, heritage, religion etc. in hopes to better shape Australias future by altering alarming statistics. The census has an aim of collecting data so that the government and Australias own organisations, businesses and people can better grapple how our demographics may reflect our actions and habits, thus being able to make an informed decisions on policy, planning etc to improve upon communities and ourselves. Moreover, it assists the government to estimate where funds should be distributed to develop initiatives and provide services within lagging areas such as education, housing and transport.
